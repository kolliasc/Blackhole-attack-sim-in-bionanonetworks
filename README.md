Author: Charalampos Kollias
This project was carried out as part of my bachelor’s thesis in Computer Science at the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki.

Biological nanonetworks constitute an innovative and rapidly evolving field in which nanotechnology meets biology with the aim of enabling communication at the molecular level. 
In these systems, information exchange is carried out through carrier molecules that propagate in space mainly through diffusion mechanisms and interact with specific receptors according to the Ligand–Receptor model.
In this way, natural biological processes are reproduced, enabling the development of bio-inspired communication models with applications in targeted therapy, biosensing technology, and micro- and nanosystems.

However, despite their significant potential, biological nanonetworks are characterized by increased complexity and sensitivity to environmental changes. 
The stochastic nature of diffusion, the limitations in binding and unbinding rates, as well as the possible presence of malicious or interfering nodes, can significantly affect the performance and reliability of communication.

This work investigates the simulation of a Blackhole attack and a corresponding countermeasure within the AnyLogic environment. 
By examining different operational scenarios, the impact of malicious activity on the flow of molecules and on system performance metrics—such as absorption rate and reception delay—was analyzed. 
The results showed that even localized interventions can cause significant degradation of communication, leading to reduced reliability and an increased probability of errors.

This study highlights the importance of integrating detection and protection mechanisms so that future biological nanonetworks can be not only functional but also resilient to malicious influences. 
Therefore, a solution is also proposed that appears to mitigate the attack. Specifically, sensors are placed at certain locations and are ready to release negative chemotactic molecules under specific conditions. 
In this way, the attack appears to be counteracted and the molecules are successfully guided toward their intended target.
